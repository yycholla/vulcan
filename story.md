# The Last Ember of Kethra

The world ended not with a bang, but with a whisper.

For a thousand years, the forge-city of Kethra had burned bright beneath the mountain — its great Heart-Furnace fed by the molten prayers of the Dwellers Below. Every sword, every lamp, every child's first skipping-stone was forged in that sacred fire.

Until the fire went out.

Elara, the youngest ember-keeper, was the first to notice. She woke to silence where there should have been the low, constant rumble of the mountain breathing. The runes on her arm — electric-blue lines pulsing in time with the Heart — had gone dark.

She ran.

The tunnels that morning were cold. The Dwellers shuffled past her with hollow eyes, their brass masks dim. No hammer sang. No anvil rang. Even the great bell at the Foundry Gate hung motionless.

At the chamber of the Heart, Elara found the old Keeper on his knees.

"It's gone," he whispered. "The last ember, child. I held it in my hands and it crumbled to ash."

Elara should have wept. Instead, she reached into her pocket and pulled out a skipping-stone — the one her father had forged for her on the day she was born. It was warm.

"You didn't hold the last ember, old man," she said, pressing the stone into his trembling hands. "I did."

And in the darkness beneath the mountain, a single spark caught, flickered, and roared back to life.
